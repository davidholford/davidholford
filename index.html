David & Jami

Our story is as old as time...

Boy and girl meet at mutual friends party, both are instantly attracted to one another. In the following days the boy and girl begin to hang out more and more frequently eventually building feelings for one another. After a couple months of this, the boy starts to see his future intertwined with the girls and asks her to be his girlfriend. The girl gladly says yes! For the next three years the two do everything together, camping trips with friends, road trips to Arizona to visit family, romantic nights spent in Denver, concerts, sports games, and finally the momentous trip to Mexico.

While in Mexico, under a clear night sky, with some of their closest friends and family, the boy mustered the liquid courage to get down on one knee and ask the girl to marry him. With tears in both their eyes she says yes! Now both the boy and girl are looking forward to their future together. With the sky as their limit, and the possibilites endless, they are ready to begin a new adventure.

Dress code?

This is an outdoor country wedding so leave your ball gown and suit at home! Put on your boots (if you have them) and dress for warm weather. We suggest that you wear close toed shoes since it will be outsite in the dirt and there are rocks around. Your more then welcome to wear shorts

Food and drink?

Who loves tacos? Good, because we will have a taco bar with all the fixings! We will have water, soda, beer, and some hard liquir. If you would like to bring something special for yourself you are more then welcome to!

Access to the Ranch?

When you come to the gate, if it is not open, the gate code is either 0230 or 0430.

Cell service?

Service can be spoty. Verizon seems to have good coverage but T-Mobile has almost none. Not sure about Att and Sprint We suggest that you write the directions and the gate code down before you leave just incase.

Directions from Laramie

From I-80 you will exit the highway at the Snowy Range Rd exit (exit 311) and head West

Continue on Snowy Range Rd and take a right following the sighns for highway 130.

Stay on highway 130 for aprox. 13 miles. You will take a left onto Big Hallow Rd. We have put up a sign indicating where to turn, but there is also a large white sign on the left at the turn that says Wild Horse Ranch

Continue on Big Hallow Rd for about 2.5 miles and take a right at the large gate. You will need to use the code above if the gate is not open

Continue straigh ahead until you reach Wild West Way and take a right. The barn will be half a mile up on the right.

About

Big Bear Ranch is owned by Jay and Kim Moore and they have graciously let us have our wedding at their property. Established in December of 2012 they recently built a beautiful barn overlooking the Snowy Mountain Range.

Where to Stay

There are multiple hotel choices in Laramie. If you plan on staying in a hotel we recommend booking ASAP. Cheyenne Frontier days happens to be at the same time and as such the hotels in the area are expecting to be filled. Jami and I will be staying at the Hilton.

For those of you that prefer the outdoors, bring your tents or campers and stay the night at Big Bear Ranch. The barn sits on 80 acres and has plenty of room for everyone.
